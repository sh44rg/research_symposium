About the Event

A Platform for Research, Ideas & Collaboration

The CSE Research Symposium is a flagship academic event organised by the Department of Computer Science & Engineering at IIT Delhi, in collaboration with the ACES-ACM Student Chapter. It brings together faculty, graduate researchers, undergraduate students, and industry practitioners under one roof to celebrate and advance cutting-edge computer science research.

Over two days, the symposium features keynote talks, faculty research presentations, graduate student talks, and poster sessions spanning four core research domains: Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning, Systems & Security, Theory & Algorithms, and Human-Computer Interaction.

The event aims to bridge the gap between academic research and real-world application, providing a forum for cross-disciplinary dialogue, mentoring, and collaboration and inspiring the next generation of researchers at IIT Delhi.

Research Tracks

Four Core Domains

The symposium is structured around four research tracks, split across two days each covering a vibrant area of computer science.

Day 1 — April 4

Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning

Covering foundation models, deep learning, reinforcement learning, NLP, computer vision, and the fundamental theory of learning algorithms. Research that shapes how machines perceive, reason, and act.

Large Language Models Computer Vision Reinforcement Learning AI Safety NLP Graph ML
Day 1 — April 4

Systems & Security

From operating systems and distributed computing to cryptography and network security. Research that builds the reliable, efficient, and secure foundations on which software and the internet run.

Operating Systems Distributed Systems Cryptography Network Security Compilers Cloud Computing
Day 2 — April 5

Theory & Algorithms

The mathematical heart of computer science — complexity theory, algorithm design, computational geometry, combinatorics, and formal methods that underpin all of computing.

Complexity Theory Approximation Algorithms Graph Algorithms Formal Verification Quantum Computing Online Algorithms
Day 2 — April 5

Human-Computer Interaction

Bridging the gap between people and computing systems — UI/UX research, accessibility, augmented reality, affective computing, and the social dimensions of technology design.

UI/UX Research Accessibility Augmented Reality Affective Computing CSCW Visualization
